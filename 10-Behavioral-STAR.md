# Behavioral & STAR

## Question 226-250: Behavioral Interview Framework

**STAR Method**: Situation, Task, Action, Result

**Q226: Biggest production incident**
- Situation: What system, what impact (scale)
- Task: What was your role
- Action: What did you do (troubleshooting steps)
- Result: Resolution time, what was learned, prevention

**Q227: Major failure and lessons**
- Be honest, specific
- Show learning
- How you prevented recurrence
- Vulnerability + growth mindset

**Q228: Conflict with manager**
- Disagreement on direction
- How you communicated
- Tried to understand their perspective
- How resolved (compromise or escalation)
- What you learned

**Q229: Conflict with peer**
- Situation: Disagreement on approach
- Tried to understand their view
- Collaborative resolution
- Maintained relationship
- Better outcome together

**Q230: Tight deadline**
- What was deadline, stakes
- How you prioritized
- What you cut vs what was core
- Communication with stakeholders
- Result (met deadline, trade-offs)

**Q231: Legacy migration**
- Old system constraints
- Why migration needed
- Approach (big bang vs gradual)
- Team coordination
- Successful migration

**Q232: Security issue**
- What vulnerability, severity
- How discovered
- Steps to fix
- Communication/disclosure
- Process improvements

**Q233: Customer escalation**
- Root cause
- How you responded
- Owned the issue
- Solution and follow-up
- Prevented recurrence

**Q234: Scaling challenge**
- System bottleneck, growth
- How you identified
- Solution (scaling strategy)
- Implementation
- Result (improved performance)

**Q235: Mentoring success**
- Who you mentored, background
- Specific coaching/feedback
- Growth they achieved
- Your role, effort
- Outcome

**Q236: Hard decision**
- Tradeoff: Cost vs quality, features vs debt
- Who it impacted
- How you decided
- Communication
- Outcome

**Q237: Missed deadline**
- Root causes (estimation, scope, blockers)
- How you communicated early
- Mitigation (what you did)
- What you learned
- Prevention for future

**Q238: Innovation example**
- Problem you identified
- Novel solution
- Implementation
- Impact
- How it changed workflows

**Q239: Ownership example**
- Initiative you led
- Went beyond scope
- Drove to completion
- Impact on team/company
- Personal growth

**Q240: Handling ambiguity**
- Unclear requirements
- How you clarified (questions, research)
- Decisions you made
- Built solution
- Validated with stakeholders

**Q241: Cost reduction**
- Problem (high costs)
- Analysis (where waste)
- Solution
- Implementation
- Measurable savings

**Q242: Automation initiative**
- Repetitive process
- Automation approach
- Implementation
- Time/cost saved
- Team adoption

**Q243: Difficult stakeholder**
- Who, why difficult
- How you communicated
- Built relationship
- Found common ground
- Successful collaboration

**Q244: Leading change**
- What changed (technology, process)
- Why it was hard
- How you drove adoption
- Resistance handling
- Successful transition

**Q245: Remote collaboration**
- Challenges (timezone, communication)
- How you overcame
- Tools/processes
- Maintained culture
- Results

**Q246: Prioritization under pressure**
- Competing demands
- How you prioritized
- Communicated trade-offs
- Results
- Did you miss anything?

**Q247: Learning new tech fast**
- What, why learn
- Approach (learning resources, practice)
- Timeline
- Applied in project
- Still learning?

**Q248: Handling burnout in team**
- Symptoms observed
- Root causes
- Actions taken (workload, support)
- Follow-up
- Team improvement

**Q249: Diversity of thought**
- Example of different perspective
- How you valued it
- Listened actively
- Better outcome
- Culture built

**Q250: Why this role?**
- What attracted you
- Alignment with career goals
- What you'll contribute
- Growth opportunity
- Long-term interest
